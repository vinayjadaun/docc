DR.DHAIVAT SHAH
(MBBS,MS,DNB,FMRF)
Dr. Dhaivat Shah is a board-certified Ophthalmologist and Vitreoretinal Surgeon hailing from Baroda, Gujarat. After completing his schooling at Rosary High School, Baroda, he finished MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery) from the Smt. NHL Municipal medical college (VS/SVP Hospital), Ahmedabad. He has a double Master’s degree in Ophthalmology, that is, Master of Surgery (MS) from the reputed C H Nagri Eye Hospital, Ahmedabad, and Diplomate of National Board (DNB) from Natboard, New Delhi. Thereafter, he pursued a long-term fellowship in Vitreoretina from the world-renowned Institute of Sankara Nethralaya, Chennai, and had an opportunity to work with the legends in the field of retina and uvea.

Currently, he is the Joint Medical Director of Choithram Netralaya, Indore. He is also the Head of the Department of Vitreoretina, Research and Academics at the institute and is thoroughly involved in DNB and Optometry training. He is also a pioneer to start ROP services in the institute and has collaborated with various international institutions and organizations for research outreach. Surgically, he has vast experience of more than 3000 simple and complex vitreoretinal surgeries, and he is acclaimed for unique techniques of secondary IOL’s, management of complex retinal detachments, and endophthalmitis. His special interests include retinal imaging and newer therapies for diabetic retinopathy, occlusive retinal disease, age-related macular degeneration and pediatric retinopathies. He has more than 50 peer-reviewed national and international publications to his name. He is also a part of the editorial team for some reputed national journals. He is fluent in English, Hindi, and Gujarati (the latter being the mother-tongue), and has a trace understanding of Bengali and Tamil.

Dr. Dhaivat firmly believes that medicine is an art of amalgamation of good clinical skills along with a human touch, hence examining the patient as a whole, establishing staunch compliance with appropriate counseling is as crucial as the disease treatment itself.

“If you can’t do great things,do little things in a great way !”
Dealing with ocular pathologies is intricate, intriguing and interesting. As Dr. Dhaivat exclaims, “Medicine for me is not just a 9:00-5:00 job. It’s a continuous life experience. To understand what a patient feels in that particular gloomy condition and to be able to share the experience along with the patient is something that makes the worth of a doctor’s real value. To share in happiness is important, but to share in distress is always more crucial and highly valued. It’s the most beautiful feeling in the world to see a person getting healed, and as a doctor, I feel truly blessed to witness that firsthand in my patients!” Here is an insight into some of the diseases and conditions Dr. Dhaivat specializes in and is passionate about.
